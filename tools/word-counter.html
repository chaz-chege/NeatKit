Understanding your stats

Reading time is calculated at 200 words per minute — the average adult silent reading speed. Academic and technical content is typically slower (150 wpm); casual reading is faster (250+ wpm).
Speaking time uses 130 words per minute, the average conversational pace. For presentations you want to allow more time — nervous speakers tend to rush, so aim for 120 wpm when planning.
Character count with vs without spaces matters for different platforms. Twitter's 280-character limit counts spaces. SMS messages count spaces. Some databases count without. Check which one applies to your use case.

What the word counter measures

The tool analyses your text in real time as you type or paste, giving you an instant breakdown of multiple metrics simultaneously. All counting happens in your browser — no text is sent anywhere.

📝
Words
Counts sequences of non-whitespace characters separated by spaces, tabs, or newlines. Hyphenated words like "well-known" count as one word. Numbers count as words.
🔤
Characters
Total characters including spaces, punctuation, and special characters. Also shown without spaces — useful for platforms that measure text density differently.
📄
Sentences & paragraphs
Sentences split on full stops, question marks, and exclamation marks. Paragraphs are separated by blank lines. Useful for checking readability and structure.
⏱️
Reading & speaking time
Estimated time to read or speak the text based on average adult reading speed (200 wpm) and average speaking pace (130 wpm).

Word and character limits by platform

A reference guide for the limits you're most likely to hit. Paste your text, check the count, and trim to fit.

🐦
X / Twitter
280 characters per tweet (including spaces). URLs count as 23 characters regardless of length. Images and polls don't count toward the limit.
💼
LinkedIn
Posts: 3,000 characters. Article headlines: 220 characters. Profile summary: 2,600 characters. Job descriptions: 5,000 characters.
📸
Instagram
Captions: 2,200 characters, but only the first 125 show before "more". Bio: 150 characters. Comments: 1,000 characters.
📧
Email subject lines
Most email clients show 40–60 characters before cutting off. Mobile shows even less — around 30. Keep subject lines under 50 characters for full visibility.
🎓
Academic writing
Essay word counts vary: 500w (short answer), 1,500–2,000w (standard essay), 4,000–6,000w (dissertation chapter), 15,000–20,000w (full dissertation).
🔍
SEO meta descriptions
Google displays approximately 155–160 characters in search results. Meta titles should be 50–60 characters. Write for the limit, not under it.

What makes a good word count for different content types?

Word count affects readability, SEO performance, and audience engagement differently depending on content type. Here is a quick guide to typical targets.

📰
Blog posts
300–500 words for short news updates. 800–1,200 words for standard posts. 1,500–2,500 words for in-depth guides. Long-form content (2,500+) tends to rank better in search.
📜
Cover letters
250–400 words is the standard advice. One page maximum. Hiring managers spend an average of 7 seconds on an initial CV scan — keep cover letters focused and short.
🎤
Speeches and presentations
At 130 wpm: 5 minutes = ~650 words, 10 minutes = ~1,300 words, 20 minutes = ~2,600 words. Always add 10–15% buffer time for pauses and audience reaction.
📱
WhatsApp / SMS
SMS: 160 characters per message (GSM encoding). After 160 characters it splits into multiple messages. Keep important information in the first 160 characters.
