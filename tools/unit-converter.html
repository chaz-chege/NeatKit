Everyday conversion reference

Click any conversion result card in the tool above to instantly set it as your target unit — no need to find it in the dropdown.
Temperature conversions are the trickiest because they involve addition, not just multiplication. The tool handles this automatically, but the formula for Celsius to Fahrenheit is: multiply by 9/5 then add 32. Fahrenheit to Celsius: subtract 32 then multiply by 5/9.
Data units in this tool use the standard SI decimal definitions (1 KB = 1,000 bytes). Some operating systems (Windows) use binary definitions (1 KB = 1,024 bytes). If your file sizes don't match exactly, this is why.

Metric vs imperial — a practical guide

🌍
Who uses metric
Almost every country in the world including Kenya, all of Europe, China, India, Australia, and Canada. The international standard for science, medicine, and most engineering.
🇺🇸
Who uses imperial
Primarily the United States. Myanmar and Liberia officially use imperial but in practice mix both. The UK uses a mix: metric for most things, miles for road distances, pints for beer.
🔬
Science always uses metric
Scientific publications, medical dosages, and engineering specifications use SI (metric) units universally regardless of the country of origin. Convert to metric for any technical work.
🏗️
Construction varies
Building materials are sold in metric in Kenya and most countries. US lumber uses imperial (2×4 inches). If importing materials or following US construction plans, watch unit conversions closely.

Frequently asked questions

There are two competing standards. SI decimal (used by storage manufacturers and this tool): 1 KB = 1,000 bytes, 1 MB = 1,000,000 bytes. Binary (used by most operating systems): 1 KiB = 1,024 bytes, 1 MiB = 1,048,576 bytes. A hard drive advertised as 1 TB (1,000,000,000,000 bytes) shows as about 931 GB in Windows, which uses binary definitions. Mac switched to decimal in 2009 so it shows the advertised size.
A nautical mile (1,852 metres) is defined as one minute of arc of latitude along any meridian. This makes it directly useful for navigation — 1 nautical mile corresponds to 1 minute of latitude on a map. Ships and aircraft still use nautical miles for this reason. Speed at sea is measured in knots (nautical miles per hour).
Mass is the amount of matter in an object (kilograms, grams). Weight is the gravitational force acting on that mass — it changes depending on gravity. On the Moon, your mass is the same but you weigh about 1/6th as much. In everyday use, people say "weight" when they mean mass. This tool converts mass units (kg, lbs, etc.) — technically mass, colloquially called weight.
All conversions (except temperature) are calculated using a base unit factor — each unit has a conversion factor to a common base (metres for length, grams for weight, etc.). The tool converts your input to the base unit then to the target unit. Temperature uses direct formulas because Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin scales have different zero points, making simple multiplication insufficient.
