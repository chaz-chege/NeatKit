How the AI detector works

This tool runs entirely in your browser. Your text is never sent to a server, stored, or logged anywhere. The analysis uses six statistical signals that consistently separate AI-generated writing from human writing, based on research into how large language models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini produce text.

📏
Sentence length uniformity
AI models tend to produce sentences of similar length throughout a passage. Human writing naturally varies — short punchy sentences alongside long, complex ones. This tool measures that variation using the coefficient of variation across all sentences.
📚
Lexical diversity
The ratio of unique words to total words in a text. AI outputs often recycle the same vocabulary patterns within a passage. Human writers tend to reach for more varied words naturally, even without conscious effort.
🔗
Transition word overuse
Words like "furthermore", "moreover", "consequently", and "it is important to note" are statistically rare in natural human writing but appear constantly in AI output. The tool checks for 24 of the most common AI transitional phrases.
🔄
Passive voice frequency
AI writing leans heavily on passive constructions ("it was demonstrated that", "this can be seen in"). Humans default to active voice. The tool scans for common passive patterns using auxiliary verb + past participle combinations.
🌫️
Vague filler language
Words like "significant", "various", "robust", "leverage", and "utilize" are high-frequency in AI output but low in meaning. They signal AI writing because models optimize for sounding thorough rather than being specific.
🔤
Sentence starter variety
AI models frequently begin consecutive sentences with "The", "This", or "These". Human writers vary their sentence openings much more. This tool tracks how many unique sentence starters appear relative to the total number of sentences.

Understanding your results

The overall AI score is a weighted combination of all six signals. No single signal is definitive — a high score on one metric doesn't mean the text is AI-generated. The tool looks for patterns across all signals together.

Score range What it means What to do
0 – 29 Strongly human-written. Writing shows natural irregularity, varied structure, and personal voice. Nothing — the writing reads as genuinely human.
30 – 49 Probably human, with some formal or repetitive patterns. Could be academic or technical writing style. Review flagged sentences. Consider varying sentence length and replacing transition words.
50 – 74 Mixed signals. May be AI-assisted, heavily edited AI output, or a very formal human writing style. Check which metrics are high and target those specifically. The sentence breakdown will show you where.
75 – 100 Likely AI-generated. Multiple strong signals are present across sentence structure, vocabulary, and phrasing. Rewrite flagged sentences in your own words. Add specific examples, personal observations, or concrete details.

Why scores can be wrong

Non-native English speakers often write in patterns that score higher than their actual human effort deserves — formal grammar instruction produces writing that looks AI-like statistically. Highly technical writing (scientific papers, legal documents, policy writing) also tends to score higher because the domain requires formal, structured language. A high score is a signal worth investigating, not a verdict.
