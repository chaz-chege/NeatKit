Tips for clean document scans

Auto-capture is your best friend. Hold the camera steady over the document and let the tool detect the edges and capture automatically — it produces more consistent results than tapping manually.
Dark background, white paper. Place your document on a dark surface — a dark desk, a folder, or dark cloth. The contrast helps the edge detection algorithm find the document borders cleanly.
Even lighting across the whole page. Shadows on one side of the document create uneven exposure that makes text harder to read. Move to a well-lit spot or turn on overhead lights before scanning.

How the document scanner works

This tool uses your device's camera to scan physical documents and convert them to PDF — entirely in the browser. The scanner uses real-time edge detection based on the Sobel operator, a computer vision algorithm that identifies sharp boundaries between your document and its background.

When auto-capture is enabled, the tool continuously analyses the camera feed and triggers a capture when it detects a stable document rectangle. The captured image is processed, perspective-corrected, and added to your document stack. Once you have all pages, convert to a single PDF with one click — no uploads required.
