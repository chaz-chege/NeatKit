Tips for reliable QR scanning

Hold steady at the right distance. Most phone cameras need the QR code to fill roughly 30–50% of the frame. Too close and the camera can't focus; too far and the code is too small to resolve. Find the sweet spot and hold still for a moment.
Upload a screenshot instead of photographing a screen. If you need to scan a QR code from another phone, website, or PDF, take a screenshot and upload it here — the result is always cleaner than photographing a screen through a camera lens.
Improve lighting if scanning fails. QR scanning struggles in low contrast conditions. Turn on a light, increase your screen brightness, or move to a better-lit area. The scanner needs clear contrast between the dark modules and the white quiet zone.

How QR code scanning works

Scanning a QR code is a computer vision process. The scanner first locates the three finder patterns — the large squares in three corners — to determine the code's position, size, and rotation. It then corrects for any perspective distortion (if the code is photographed at an angle), samples each module (grid square) as black or white, and reads the encoded bit pattern.

This tool uses the ZXing library (Zebra Crossing), an open-source barcode reading library originally developed by Google, running entirely in your browser. Your camera feed and uploaded images are processed locally — nothing is sent to any server.

QR code safety — what to watch for

QR codes themselves are harmless data containers, but they can link to harmful content just like any URL. Here is how to stay safe when scanning unknown QR codes.

👁️
Check the URL before tapping
After scanning, your phone shows the URL before opening it. Read it carefully. Look for misspellings of known domains (paypa1.com, g00gle.com) or unfamiliar shortened URLs that hide the real destination.
📌
Beware of sticker tampering
A common scam places a fraudulent QR code sticker over a legitimate one in public places — parking meters, restaurant tables, shop windows. If a QR code sticker looks like it's been placed over something, don't scan it.
🔐
WiFi QR codes and passwords
WiFi QR codes contain the network password in plain text inside the code. Anyone who scans the code can read the password. Don't display WiFi QR codes in public if you want to restrict access to a private network.
💳
Payment QR fraud
Fraudsters replace legitimate M-Pesa or payment QR codes with their own. Always verify the recipient name shown after scanning a payment QR before completing any transaction.
