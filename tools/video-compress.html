Why target size beats quality sliders

Nobody thinks in quality levels. "Medium quality" means nothing when your actual problem is "this won't send on WhatsApp." Target size mode flips the question: tell the tool where it needs to go, and it calculates the bitrate required to get there — one encode, done.
Lower resolution first, then bitrate. If the target size is very small relative to your video duration, drop the resolution to 720p or 480p before compressing. A lower-resolution video at a reasonable bitrate looks better than a full-resolution video starved of data.
WhatsApp's actual limit is 16MB for video sharing (not the 2GB storage limit). Videos above 16MB will fail to send. Telegram's free tier allows 50MB. Gmail attachments cap at 25MB. The presets match these real limits.

How the bitrate calculation works

Most video compressors hide their bitrate settings and make you guess. This tool shows you the numbers and explains them.

🧮
The formula
Target bitrate = (target size in bits − audio data) ÷ duration in seconds. Audio is reserved first (128kbps default) since audio quality degrades badly at low bitrates. The rest goes to video.
📊
What bitrate means
Bitrate is data per second. Higher bitrate = more data = better quality. 1080p video typically needs 4–8 Mbps to look good. 720p needs 2–4 Mbps. Below 500 kbps, blocking and blur artefacts become visible.
⚙️
H.264 encoding
The tool encodes to H.264 (libx264) with the calculated bitrate using the "medium" preset. H.264 is universally compatible — plays on every phone, tablet, smart TV, and browser without needing special codecs.
🔒
100% private
Everything runs in your browser using FFmpeg compiled to WebAssembly. Your video never leaves your device. Open DevTools → Network and verify no upload request fires during compression.

Common use cases

💬
WhatsApp video sharing
WhatsApp caps video at 16MB. A 1-minute 1080p recording from a modern phone is typically 150–300MB. Select the WhatsApp preset and drop to 720p for a good size/quality balance.
📧
Email attachments
Gmail and Outlook cap attachments at 25MB. Short video clips for client presentations, training videos, or event highlights can be compressed to fit without using cloud sharing links.
🌐
Website video uploads
Most website builders (Squarespace, Wix, WordPress) have upload limits. Background videos for hero sections should be under 5–10MB — use the Custom preset and drop resolution to 720p.
📋
Form and portal submissions
Job application portals, university submissions, and government forms with video requirements often cap uploads at 10–50MB. Use the Custom preset and type the exact limit.
✈️
Telegram free tier
Telegram's free plan allows files up to 50MB. Longer videos for group chats, tutorials, or event recordings can be compressed to fit. The Telegram preset targets exactly this limit.
💾
Storage and archiving
Compress video archives before uploading to Google Drive or Dropbox to save quota. A folder of event recordings at 720p 2Mbps is a fraction of the original size without visible quality loss on most screens.
