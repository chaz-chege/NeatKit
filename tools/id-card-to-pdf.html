Tips for the best ID card PDF

Good lighting is everything. Take the photo near a window with natural light. Avoid flash — it creates glare on laminated cards and makes text hard to read.
Fill the frame. Get as close as possible so the card fills most of the camera view. The tool will handle alignment and cropping, but starting with a clean, close shot gives the best result.
Front and back on one PDF. Add both sides as separate images — they'll become separate pages in the PDF. This is the standard format required by most institutions when submitting ID copies.

How the ID card to PDF tool works

This tool converts photos of ID cards, driving licences, student cards, and similar documents into a clean, properly formatted PDF — entirely in your browser. No uploads, no server, no account required.

You can upload a photo from your gallery or use your device's camera to capture the card directly. The tool processes the image locally using the HTML5 Canvas API and PDF-lib to generate a downloadable PDF sized and formatted for document submission.

Where you'll need an ID card PDF

🏛️
Government portals
eCitizen, KRA iTax, NHIF, NSSF, and other government platforms require ID copies as PDF when registering or making applications. Phone photos won't upload — PDFs do.
🏦
Bank account opening
Most banks and SACCOs require a scanned copy of your national ID or passport as part of KYC (Know Your Customer) documentation — usually as a PDF.
💼
Job applications
Many online job applications and HR onboarding forms ask for a copy of your national ID. Convert your phone photo to a clean PDF before attaching.
🎓
University applications
KUCCPS and university direct applications require national ID or birth certificate copies. Student portals typically accept PDF only, not image files.
🏥
Insurance and medical
Health insurance registration and medical scheme applications often require ID copies as part of beneficiary or member registration documentation.
📋
Rental agreements
Landlords and letting agents commonly request a copy of a tenant's national ID as part of the lease agreement process before handing over keys.

Photographing your ID for best results

The quality of your ID PDF depends almost entirely on the quality of the photo. Here is what makes the difference between a clear, accepted submission and one that gets rejected for being illegible.

☀️
Use natural light
Place the card near a window. Indirect natural light gives even, shadow-free illumination. Avoid direct sunlight which creates harsh shadows, and avoid indoor fluorescent lighting which causes colour casts.
📵
No flash
Flash creates white glare spots on laminated cards that can make text completely unreadable. Turn flash off and rely on ambient light instead.
📐
Flat and straight
Place the card on a flat, dark surface. Hold the phone directly above it, parallel to the card. Angling the camera causes perspective distortion that makes text harder to read.
🔍
Check before converting
Zoom into the preview to verify all text — especially the ID number, name, and date of birth — is sharp and readable before generating the PDF. Re-shoot if anything is blurry.
