Tips before you convert

Compress large images first. A PDF made from 10 uncompressed phone photos can be 40–60MB. Run them through the Image Compressor before converting and the PDF will be a fraction of that size.
Drag to reorder before converting. The page order in the PDF matches the grid. Rearrange thumbnails first — you can't reorder pages inside the PDF after it's created without a separate tool.
Use A4 for official documents. A4 is the standard paper size in Kenya and most of the world. Letter is the US standard. When in doubt, choose A4.

How images to PDF conversion works

This tool converts your images to a PDF entirely in your browser using PDF-lib, an open-source JavaScript library for creating and modifying PDF files. Your images are read locally using the FileReader API and embedded into the PDF document without ever leaving your device.

Each image becomes one page in the PDF. The tool handles sizing — fitting or filling the page — by calculating the scale factor needed to make the image fill the target page dimensions while maintaining aspect ratio. The resulting PDF is generated as a binary file in memory and then triggered as a browser download.

📄
Paper sizes
A4 (595×842pt, standard internationally), Letter (612×792pt, US standard), or Auto which sizes each page to fit the image exactly with no borders or padding.
🖼️
Image fit modes
Fit page scales the image to fill the page while preserving aspect ratio with white borders. Fill page stretches to cover the full page. Actual size places the image at its native pixel dimensions.
↕️
Drag to reorder
Drag thumbnails in the grid to reorder pages before converting. The final PDF reflects the grid order exactly. Remove individual images using the × on each thumbnail.
🔒
100% private
No uploads. No server. PDF-lib runs entirely in the browser. Your images and the generated PDF never leave your device.

When to convert images to PDF

🏛️
Official document submissions
Government portals, banks, HELB, universities — most require documents as a single PDF. Convert your phone photos of certificates, IDs, or transcripts into a compliant submission.
📑
Scanned document pages
Scanned pages saved as separate JPEGs from a phone or scanner can be combined into one multi-page PDF — contracts, agreements, medical records, receipts.
📧
Email attachments
A single PDF is easier to attach and open than five separate image files. Combine photos of a multi-page document before emailing to a client or employer.
🧾
Expense and receipt records
Photograph receipts throughout the month and compile into a monthly expense PDF for accounting, reimbursement claims, or tax records.
📒
Handwritten notes
Photograph handwritten lecture notes, meeting notes, or whiteboards and convert to a PDF for sharing with colleagues or storing alongside digital files.
📸
Photo portfolios
Compile a selection of photos into a PDF portfolio for sharing or printing. Use Auto page size to let each image fill its own page without borders.
