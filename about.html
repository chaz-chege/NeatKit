Tools that actually work.

Neatkit is a collection of free, browser-based utility tools built for anyone who just needs to get something done — without signing up, without uploading files to a stranger's server, and without hitting a paywall halfway through.

Why Neatkit exists

Most online tools are built around a trap: they advertise as free, get you halfway through a task, then ask for your email, a subscription, or money to download the result. It's a frustrating experience that wastes time and feels like a bait-and-switch.

Neatkit was built to be the opposite of that. Every tool on this site runs entirely in your browser. Your files, text, and data stay on your device — nothing is uploaded, stored, or processed on any server. There are no accounts to create, no limits on how many times you can use a tool, and no features hidden behind a paywall.

The site is supported by display advertising, which is what keeps it free for everyone.

Who built this

Neatkit is an independent project built and maintained by a single developer based in Nairobi, Kenya. It started from a simple frustration: needing to compress an image or convert a PDF and hitting paywalls on every tool that came up in search results.
